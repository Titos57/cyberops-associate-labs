### Step 1b - Three

**CompTIA Security+** is an entry-level  certification, vendor-neutral and covers network security, threats, vulnerabilities, incident response, and compliance. The exam contains up to 90
questions and requires a score above 750, with a cost of $404. Prepares you for the job of a security analyst, system administrator, and SOC analyst.
**Cisco CyberOps Associate** is a vendor-specific certification that covers security monitoring, attack identification, security policies, and network security intrusion analysis. The exam
is up to 120 minutes with a cost of $300. Prepares you for the job of a SOC analyst.
**Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)** covers identifying, scanning, and testing security system vulnerabilities. The certification process consists of 125 questions with a 4-hour time limit and
requires two years of work experience or completion of official EC-Council training, with a cost of $3,500. Prepares you for the job of an ethical hacker-Pentester professional.

## Part 2 - Investigate positions available within cybersecurity

### Step 2a - Job Listings in the Last Two Weeks
A search for SOC Analyst positions on Glassdoor within Greece returned 6 new listings posted within the last 
two weeks. The limited number of listings reflects the relatively small size of the Greek cybersecurity job 
market compared to larger European markets.

### Step 2b - Salary range
The top 10 listings reviewed did not publicly disclose salary information, which is common practice in the 
Greek job market. Based on industry research, entry-level cybersecurity roles in Greece typically range from 
€800–€1,200 monthly.

### Step 2c - Required qualifications
They require 2+ years of experience in a SOC analyst role, a strong understanding of SIEM tools, excellent written and verbal communication in English with the team, and relevant industry certifications like CISSP,
C and Python development skills, and a bachelor's degree in a related field of CS or engineering.

### Step 2d - Certifications required by employers
Many industry job listings require certifications like GCIH, GCFA, CISSP, and OSCP. 

### Step 2e - Correlation with Part 1 Certifications
The CISSP certification seems to be a common requirement for a security analyst job position.

### Step 2f - Legal Hacking Practice Platforms
Several platforms allow users to legally practice penetration testing and hacking skills in controlled 
environments. TryHackMe (tryhackme.com) offers guided learning paths and challenges across multiple difficulty 
levels. Hack The Box (hackthebox.com) provides realistic machine-based challenges used by professional security 
researchers.
